Customer Retention Rate

What is Customer Retention Rate ?

The percentage of customers who continue paying the company after the first purchase.

How to calculate CRR?

CRR = E—N, (divided by) S x 100

where,
E is existing customers at the end of a period.
N is new customers acquired during a period.
S is customers acquired at the start of a period.

Important of CRR

Customer Retention Rate informs businesses about whether they are truly growing or not. Growth = more customers stay while you continue to add more.

Tips to increase Customer Retention Rate

Make it a point to deliver more than you what the user expects
Clear up bugs as soon as they are reported
Focus on making the UX as smooth and intuitive as possible
Conduct surveys to understand if there are any usability barriers
Keep community events like webinars and meetups

Burn Rate

What is Burn Rate?

Burn rate can be
Net burn, the amount a company loses monthly.
Gross burn, the company’s monthly operating cost.

Why Net Burn Rate is important?

Shows whether your current revenue is substantial enough.
Shows how long you can sail before you run out of money.

To calculate Net Burn Rate

Net Burn Rate = Cash/Capital (divided by) Losses per month

Tips to decrease Burn Rate

Try to merge or retire activities that require more money to be run.
Look for free alternatives for any of the tools you use to work/sell.
Try to bring in more qualified leads to compensate for the burn rate.
Alternatively, considering bootstrapping or raising funds.

Customer Engagement Score

What is Customer Engagement Score?

Measures how well existing customers engage with your product/service’s trial or freemium plans.

Why is Customer Engagement Score (CES) important?

A product/service succeeds when more users engage with it continuously.
A lesser CES indicates potential user churn, and also friction within the experience of the product. .

How to calculate Customer Engagement Score?

Identify the events to track, that would count as ‘engagement’
Label metrics that can help you identify if a user has completed a task, dropped out, and has come back for an encore.

A simpler method:
Label every event
Measure the usage frequency of these, for a single user.
Multiple the average frequency with the number of events.
Compare this score with scores of other users.

Tips to enhance Customer Engagement Score

Send occasional, apt emails highlighting product features or offers.
Make your website copy reflect the overall value of your product.
Consider extending the free trial for some power users.
Customize pricing plans based on users’ needs/behaviors.
Address bugs and issues as soon as users report them.
Make yourself available so that customers can reach out to you.

Viral Coefficient

What is Viral Coefficient?

The number of new users that a customer brings in on average. This metric does not account for mere referrals, but for leads that convert.

Why is the Viral Coefficient important?

Indicates a company’s ability to grow, exponentially or not.
It is correlated to the quality of your product.
That is, the better the product, the higher the Viral Coefficient.
Viral products are ones that have been positively received by many, many users.
Thus, it also helps measure the effectiveness of word-of-mouth strategies.
.

How to calculate Viral Coefficient?

Viral Coefficient = (C*R*CR)/100

Take the total number of customers (C)
Multiply it with the average number of referrals (R)
Multiply the result with the average conversion rate (CR)
Divide this result by 100

Tips to achieve a higher Viral Coefficient

Make your product eligible for virality, be it through some quirky marketing or some out-of-the-box feature.
Make your product as scalable as you can.
Have a referral program that gives existing customers an incentive to bring in more users.


Net Promoter Score (NPS)

What is Net Promoter Score (NPS)?

The metric that lets you measure customer satisfaction and loyalty. It puts them into three types: passive, detractors, and promoters.

Why is it important?

Shows how well a customer would recommend your product
0 means they’d never
10 means they’re very likely
Overall, the higher your NPS, the more satisfied the customer is.

How to calculate your Net Promoter Score?

Take surveys— Ask them to directly rate how likely they are to tell someone about your product
Segregate— Detractors are 0-6, Passives are 7-8, and Promoters are 9-10
Get your overall NPS by subtracting the percentage of detractors from that of the promoters.
% of Detractors (minus) % of Promoters = Overall NPS

Customer Lifetime Value

What is Customer Lifetime Value?

The value added by a customer over their lifetime to a business. Deriving value from an existing customer is better than acquiring a new one.

How to Calculate CLV?

CLV is the total amount of money a customer will pay to a company during their lifetime.
Formula: CLV = ARPU/Revenue or Customer Churn Rate
ARPU or Average Revenue Per User is the average amount of money paid by a customer.
Customer Churn Rate is the rate at which a customer stops making recurring purchases.

Importance of the Metric:

Customers are quite costly to acquire, and a customer becomes valuable only when they stay with a business for long. Which means, the higher the CLV, the greater the profits.
A growing CLV indicates a better retention of customers and a move toward sustainable operations.

Average Revenue Per Account (ARPA)

What is Average Revenue Per Account (ARPA)?

Average Revenue Per User (ARPU), it is the average of the revenue generated by every account, on a monthly or yearly basis.

Why is ARPA important?

ARPA allows companies to identify the revenue generation and per-unit growth of a product.
It also helps in predicting overall growth of the product, thus informing future strategies.

How to calculate ARPA?

($) Total monthly/yearly recurring revenue / (#) total accounts = ($) Average Revenue Per Account (ARPA).
You can modify this formula to get other scores, such as the Average Revenue per New Account and the Average Revenue per Existing Account.

Lead Velocity Rate

What is Lead Velocity Rate?

Shows the increase in qualified leads on a monthly basis in real-time. Helps predict future revenue scales in a consistent manner.

Why is Lead Velocity Rate important?

($) Total monthly/yearly recurring revenue / (#) total accounts = ($) Average Revenue Per Account (ARPA).
You can modify this formula to get other scores, such as the Average Revenue per New Account and the Average Revenue per Existing Account.

How to calculate Lead Velocity Rate?

Take the number of qualified leads for the current month (M1)
Subtract last month’s number from that(M2)
Divide the result with the last month’s number
Multiply by 100
LVR = (M1 ⁠— M2) / M2*100

Tips to increase Lead Velocity Rate

Be proactive and have sales team work and save up leads before any seasonal dips
Bring in a loyalty program and have existing customers recommend your product
Have seasonal offers to entice other, under-budget users


Monthly Recurring Revenue

What is Monthly Recurring Revenue (MRR)?

It is the measure of a business’s predictable revenue earned every month.

Why is MRR important?

To forecast finances
MRR is essentially a stable metric thanks to subscription models, making financial projections more accurate.
To measure growth
A month-on-month increase of your MRR is a clear indicator of a thriving business.
A declining MRR is a direct signal for employing better strategies.

How to calculate MRR?

Average Revenue Per Account * Total Number of Accounts that Month = MRR

Types of MRR

New MRR ⁠— the MRR given by new customers
Add-on MRR ⁠— the MRR given by existing customers, by buying additional features.
Churn MRR — MRR lost from cancellations and downgrades

Tips to increase MRR

Have your sales team close more qualified leads
Have variable pricing schemes and charge users based on their engagement levels
Have seasonal offers that can interest more users into buying your product
Consider decreasing your burn rate


10.Revenue Churn Rate

What is Revenue Churn Rate?

Measures the rate at which revenue is lost due to churn in customers or downgrades in subscription plans. Aka MRR Churn Rate.

Why is Revenue Churn Rate important?

Helps track churn rate between high and low spenders
Helps identify which pricing plan/ customer segment is more valuable

How to calculate Revenue Churn Rate?

Take the revenue lost over a period (month/quarter/year)
Divide it with the revenue in the beginning of this period
Multiply by 100
Revenue Churn Rate = Lost Revenue at the end of a period / Revenue in the beginning

Tips to decrease Revenue Churn Rate

Have a variable pricing scheme
Make it easier for users with lower budgets to sign up
Conduct frequent surveys for usability issues
Ask users if there is something vitally important that is missing, and work on that
Address bugs and minor issues as soon as possible
Don’t change your pricing strategies often

Average Resolution Time

What is Average Resolution Time?

The average time it takes for an organization to resolve a customer’s issue.

Why is Average Resolution Time important?

It is a better indicator of your overall customer satisfaction as it shows how fast you act upon a certain ticket.

How to Calculate Average Resolution Time?

First, select a period ⁠— a month or a quarter Take the total time spent on resolution Divide it with the total number of tickets Total time / Number of tickets = Average Resolution Time

Tips to improve your Average Resolution Time

Prioritize customer success and invest in better training Use CRM tools to streamline tickets Empower reps so that they can resolve issues quicker Look for recurring (product) issues and fix them permanently


Customer churn

What is Customer Churn?

The percentage of customers that have not paid for your product during a certain time frame

Why is Customer Churn Important?

At the very least, it takes 5x more money to acquire a new customer than to retain one. Managing customer churn would help you: Save customer acquisition costs Focus on long-term retention and recurring profits Increasing profitability with lesser efforts Understand users’ pain-points

To Calculate Customer Churn

Select a time period, generally a month or a quarter Note down the no. of new customers during this period Note down the no. of customers lost during this period Divide Acquired Customers with Lost Customers, and multiply that answer with 100. (Acquired Customers / Lost Customers) * 100

Tips to reduce Customer Churn

Invest in training your support and sales reps Make a great first impression with customers Open up multiple avenues for user feedback Constantly fix issues in your product

Annual Recurring Revenue

What is Annual Recurring Revenue?

The total revenue earned for a product or service on a yearly basis.

Why is Annual Recurring Revenue important?

It highlights the overall health of an organization. A business with less or no yearly recurring revenue would need to reconsider strategies to stay afloat. On the other hand, above-expected revenue can motivate you for bigger projects

How to calculate Annual Recurring Revenue?

Take the average cost of the product/service Multiply it with the number of paying customers Multiply this result with 12 ARR = (Cost of Product or Service) x (# of customers) x (12 Months) P.S. Revenue is the total money earned whereas Profits are the surplus after subtracting business expenses.


Product Qualified Leads

What is Product Qualified Leads (PQL)?

Any potential customer who has witnessed the value of your product (typically, through the freemium model) and is ready to start paying.

How to identify a PQL

Product Qualified Leads are the ones who: Have been on the free trial for a long period of time active in the community/forums use most of your features use your product for crucial tasks

Turning a PQL into a customer

Nudges at the right time can motivate them to start paying. These include: Let them know that paid customers have no limits
Track their features’ usage & send them badges
Highlight the differences of certain features when paid
Consult on how a paid plan can boost their productivity

Importance of PQLs

When it comes to closing deals, Product Qualified Leads are easier to convince. This makes them a better metric than sales/marketing qualified leads, as the conversion rate is higher and much more predictable.


Customer Acquisition Cost

What is Customer Acquisition Cost?

The cost required to convert a potential lead into a paying customer. The lesser your CAC is in the long run, the more profits you can make.

How to Calculate CAC?

Customer Acquisition Cost = Cost of Sales/Marketing Efforts (divided by) Number of New Paying/Acquired Customers

Importance of this metric -

Customer acquisition is a long, tedious process at the beginning stages of an organization. But slowly,
under a good acquisition strategy, word spreads about you and more leads come at your door.
Ultimately, CAC helps you understand where your marketing/sales strategies are effective or not.
